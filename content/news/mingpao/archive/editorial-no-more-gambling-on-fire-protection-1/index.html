Editorial : No More Gambling on Fire Protection

圖1之1
prevnext

【明報專訊】THE CATASTROPHIC NO. 5 ALARM FIRE at Wang Fuk Court has cast doubt on whether the scaffolding netting used in the maintenance works met fire-safety standards. Even more bewildering was the decision to seal windows with polystyrene boards, despite their obvious flammability. The tragedy exposes a "gamblers' culture" in the trade and strengthens the case for legislation with real bite—penalties strong enough to reset industry practice.

The blaze burned for more than a day and, as of last night, had claimed at least 83 lives and injured 78 others, plunging the city into grief. The extraordinary speed of the fire's spread prompts legitimate questions about whether the contractor used any fire-retardant materials. Preliminary investigations by the Police and the Fire Services Department found that many windows and ventilation openings in the estate's blocks had, astonishingly, been sealed with polystyrene boards. Their flammability is basic knowledge; any registered contractor or supervising consultant should have understood the risks. The episode reflects blatant disregard for fire safety.

Industry insiders say that oversight of routine building-renovation works is far more relaxed than that for new construction sites. Most safety officers are part-timers, inspections are perfunctory, and contractors prioritise cost and convenience. Take the use of foam boards as an example: they are lightweight, easy to cut, and far cheaper than fire-resistant materials or metal sheeting—which may well explain why they were used so liberally around Wang Fuk Court. The issue of non-retardant scaffold netting stems from the same problem: cost trumping safety. Industry figures add that fire-retardant netting costs about one-third more than standard netting and wears out faster, prompting some contractors to "take the gamble".

Yesterday (27 November), the government announced that the Buildings Department and the Housing Bureau will conduct proactive site inspections and sample-test protective netting, a move that immediately prompted some estates with ongoing facade works to remove parts of their sheeting. How widespread this "try-your-luck" avoidance of fire-retardant materials in the industry is a question that should worry everyone.

At present, no law requires fire-retardant materials for scaffolding or netting in facade repair works, nor is there any ban on flammable materials such as polystyrene. Resistance from the industry and vested interests appears to be hindering legislative oversight.

In theory, the Buildings Department may prosecute and impose disciplinary action under the Buildings Ordinance, and the practice notes are said to hold a "special legal status". In reality, non-compliance is not an offence in itself. Even when the Ordinance is breached, penalties typically amount to tens or hundreds of thousands of dollars in fines, with little prospect of imprisonment.

For contractors and the professionals meant to oversee them, the calculation is stark: unless a project fails catastrophically and causes deaths, criminal liability is unlikely, and non-compliance with codes carries minimal consequences. Such "rules of the game" inevitably encourage a culture of complacency—the belief that disasters are unlikely and fire safety can be set aside.

The government has proposed consulting the industry on replacing bamboo scaffolds with metal ones. Metal frames are more durable and more fire-resistant, so the shift is welcome; but bamboo itself does not ignite easily. Without mandating fire-retardant netting and banning polystyrene boards and other flammable materials, a switch to metal scaffolding alone is plainly insufficient. The authorities must rectify industry practices, tighten the law to mandate fire-safety requirements for temporary works, and introduce stringent penalties to deter contractors from gambling with safety.

明報社評2025.11.28：端正業界僥倖文化 立法規管防火要求

大埔宏福苑五級大火，棚網是否符合防火標準固然惹人懷疑，以發泡膠板圍封窗戶，無視易燃危險，更匪夷所思。事件令人關注業界是否存在「僥倖文化」，當局有必要立法加強規管，以具有阻嚇力的刑罰，端正行業文化。

宏福苑大火燒了一日多，截至昨晚已知至少83人死、78人傷，全城哀痛。大火蔓延之快超乎尋常，維修工程承辦商有否採用阻燃物料，惹人懷疑；警方及消防初步調查發現，屋苑大廈不少窗戶及通風位置，竟然都被發泡膠板封住，更讓人震驚。發泡膠易燃是常識，作為註冊承建商以及負責把關的工程顧問，理應了解風險所在。事態反映有人罔顧防火安全。

行內人士稱，一般大廈維修工程的監管，較新建地盤「寬鬆」，安全主任絕大部分為兼職，檢查走過場，承辦商則往往以成本和方便優先。以使用發泡膠板為例，其輕身、容易裁剪、價錢比使用防火物料或鐵板便宜一截，不排除這正是宏福苑維修工程大量使用的原因。有關棚架棚網物料防燃的問題，說到底同樣是錢作怪。行內人士指出，阻燃網成本較一般保護網高三分之一，損耗也比較快，導致有人「鋌而走險」。

政府昨天宣布，屋宇署和房屋局將主動巡查地盤，抽樣測試大廈保護網，馬上有其他正在進行外牆工程的屋苑拆除部分棚網。業界「博懵」不用阻燃物料，情况究竟有多普遍，實在令人關注。

現時未有法例規管大廈外牆維修工程的棚架棚網必須使用阻燃物料，甚至沒有規定不得使用發泡膠等易燃物料。業界及利益持份者的阻力，似乎正在妨礙立法規管。

雖說屋宇署可按《建築物條例》提出檢控及紀律處分，當局註明相關指引具有「特殊的法律地位」，但不遵從守則本身並非罪行。即使違反《建築物條例》，一般也只是罰款數萬至數十萬元，判囚機率相當低。

對承辦商和負責把關的專業人士而言，只要工程沒出事（例如搞出人命），就不會捲入刑事訴訟，不遵從守則也不會有事。這樣的「遊戲規則」，必然助長業界僥倖心理，以為出事機率極低，將防火安全拋諸腦後。

政府表示將與業界磋商全面改用金屬棚架取代竹棚。鑑於金屬棚架比較靈活及耐燃，改用是一件好事，不過，竹棚本身也不容易起火；不去硬性規定棚網要用阻燃物料，以及禁用發泡膠板等易燃物，單是改用金屬棚架，顯然不足。當局必須端正行業文化，修例訂明臨時工程防火要求，引入嚴厲罰則，令業界不敢心存僥倖。
